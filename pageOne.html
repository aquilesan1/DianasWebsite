Kyoto, Japan

This might be one of Japan's most historic cities, and it's definitely my personal favourite. It has so much history and beauty. The jetlag ended up working in our favour, and we were able to discover some of Kyoto's most famous land marks just as the sun was rising around 5am. This meant lots of walking, and even more memories were made before noon. All of Japan is very friendly and polite, but I found that the kindest people over all were in Kyoto.

Jupiter, Florida

This is a beautiful sunny community where golf carts and boats might be used more often than cars; in many cases. The canal can be driven through and has little nooks of sand banks where you can stop and lay in the middle of the river. Florida has so much wildlife that blends into the cities; I was lucky enough to swim with a manatee the day we were out exploring Jupiter. This makes it one of my favourite experiences as a trip.

San Antonio, Texas

This city is so full of life and culture. The main shops and restaurants are nestled on the "riverwalk" where you can see centuries of old architecture mixed with modern day life. The mix of american and mexican heritage also play a huge role in the city's flavours.

Stanley, Hong Kong

Visiting was a whirlwind experience with so much to see and so many new things. This may be one of the world's busiest and more expensive places but you can still find hidden gems of nature amongst the city. There's so much to eat and do, this city truly never sleeps. There are pockets of specialty shops and areas for any kind of interest.

Jinotepe, Nicaragua

This is where my mom was born and raised. I did also live there for a year as a baby. Nicaragua is a beautiful country with so much biodiversity and blossoming nature. Jinotepe in particular is a smaller city, where everyone knows each other and neighbors stop by your door just to check in on their way to the market. Every time I am visiting family, I feel like there are always so many new things to see and do in this tropical escape.
